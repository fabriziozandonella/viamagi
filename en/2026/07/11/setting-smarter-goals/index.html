Setting Goals with the SMARTER Method

We often set goals that feel more like “orders” we give ourselves rather than true aspirations. How many times have we started the year with a list of resolutions, only to find ourselves feeling drained and directionless after just a few weeks?

The problem is not our lack of willpower—it is our method.

It is not enough for a goal to be logical; it must be alive. Because of this, today I want to share with you an evolution of the classic planning model: The SMARTER Method (I added “ER” to the classic SMART).

The Structure of a Mindful Goal

To transform an abstract desire into a concrete reality, every single goal should pass through these seven criteria:

1. Specific (S)

A vague goal produces vague results. Instead of saying “I want to be successful,” define exactly what that means to you. Specificity is the first step toward focusing your intention.

  • Question: What exactly do I want to achieve?

2. Measurable (M)

You must have clear indicators to understand if you are on the right track—indicators that guide you toward achieving the goal.

  • Question: How will I know when I have reached the goal?

3. Actionable (A)

Ambition is noble, but it must rest on solid ground. A goal must be achievable with your current resources or with those you can acquire.

  • Question: Do I have the skills or the means to make this goal a reality?

4. Relevant (R)

Not all goals deserve your time. A relevant goal is one that truly shifts the balance in your life, moving you closer to your long-term vision.

  • Question: Why is this goal important to me right now?

5. Time-bound (T)

Without a deadline, a goal is just a dream. Time creates the necessary creative and productive tension that drives us to action.

  • Question: By when do I want to complete this transformation?

6. Ethical (E)

This is where the SMARTER method elevates itself. A goal must not only “work,” it must be right. It must honor your core values and never compromise your integrity.

  • Question: Is this goal in alignment with what I deeply believe in?

7. Resonant (R)

The goal must strike a chord deep within you. There must be a desire driven by love and self-actualization behind the commitment, not just a sense of duty.

  • Question: Does this destination make my heart beat, or does it merely answer an external expectation?

From Theory to Practice: An Example of Transmutation

Let’s look at how a common desire can be transformed using the SMARTER framework.

Initial Desire: “I want to change jobs/companies”

Translated using our SMARTER framework:

Specific (S)

The goal must be as clear as a snapshot photograph. “Changing jobs” is too vague for the brain.

  • What is the exact role you want to step into? (Are you staying in your current field or turning toward something completely different?)
  • What kind of environment do you want to work in? (A multinational corporation, a local business, full remote work, a specific sector?)
  • What will you concretely do during a typical day?

Measurable (M)

How will you track, step by step, that you are closing in on your goal?

  • What are the numerical or tangible indicators? (e.g., Number of targeted applications sent per week, number of interviews landed, starting salary or targeted economic bracket.)

Actionable / Achievable (A)

It should challenge you, but it must remain sustainable and realistic relative to your current resources.

  • What are the very first concrete actions you can take this week? (e.g., Updating your resume with a specific angle, optimizing LinkedIn, mapping out 10 target companies.)
  • Do you already possess the necessary skills, or is there a small gap to bridge?

Relevant (R)

Why is this change crucial for you at this exact moment in your life?

  • How will this new job improve the quality of your daily life?
  • What happens if you do not change jobs in the coming months?

Time-bound (T)

Without a deadline, the mind procrastinates.

  • What is the exact date by which you want to sign the new contract or begin this new career adventure?
  • Let’s set milestones: by when must the preparation phase (resume/research) be finalized?

Ethical (E)

Alignment with your deepest values. If a goal conflicts with what you believe in, you will subconsciously sabotage the result.

  • What are the 3 core values that the new company or role must respect? (e.g., Freedom, meritocracy, respect for personal time, transparency.)
  • Is there anything you would never be willing to do or accept in this new environment?

Resonant (R)

Connecting with your “feeling”—the emotional and energetic impact.

  • When you visualize having already landed this job and see yourself in the new environment, what physical and emotional sensation do you feel in your body?
  • Does this goal give you a surge of enthusiasm (even if mixed with a bit of fear), or does it feel like a heavy, draining “duty”?
